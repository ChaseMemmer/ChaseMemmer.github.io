Prosthetic Arm Research

Aug 2022 - Current

My current pet project.

Many of the past projects have been aimed at developing a specific sub-skill, so my goal with this project is to start to combine skills into a whole product. That being said, the first phase will still focus on developing my coding abilities. Once finished1, the project has direct and easily detailed extensions (aka, more degrees of freedom, more "human-like" aesthetic) which I hope to pursue, even while working.


In essence, I am researching and attempting to design both the hardware and software of a prosthetic arm with three degrees of freedom (corresponding to elbow, wrist flexion/extension and 4-finger grasping). Software-wise, I'm aiming for real-time filtering and processing of EMG data through several different machine learning techniques. I'll select the best from those. Most of the fundamental research is done and I'm beginning to source parts for a preliminary budget scope so I order parts mid-September.

ABEX Satellite Project

Jan 2022 - Dec 2022

Space-grade Energy Harvesting System

I'm the team lead among five students (supervised by a professor) whose goal is to design and manufacture an Energy Harvest System (EHS) for a satellite. The state of Alabama is attempting to be the first state to send a fully functional satellite, that has been designed by only students, into space. Along with six other teams (at other colleges, for other parts of the satellite), my team is following NASA's published standards and are managed by a team of System Engineering graduate students.


My responsibilities include designing all the hardware, management of the other four members, budgeting and sourcing/ordering parts. I'm also the first point of contact for issues in the software, of which there are many. The EHS consist of solar panels, a PCB to control/optimize the harvested energy, a PCB to control battery charging/discharging and a DC-DC conversion board for the three rails other satellite instruments will draw from. Of course, everything is in-house and digitized for communication with the Command Center. There are also safety sub-systems for various stages of satellite deployment.

DEA Project

Feb 2022 - May 2022

Start-to-finish mechatronics project

Dielectric Elastomer Actuators (DEAs) are the current leading technology that could replace human muscle tissue. However, they're also extremely robust as a normal actuator, with the main downside being the high operating voltage. During the semester long project, I researched, modelled and built a functioning set of modular DEAs by myself. The modelling was done in LTSpice and then designed to KiCAD.


The DEA itself has an oscillator powered by two 9V batteries, a current amplifier then several methods to increase the voltage. The final voltage was ~1600V. Note, the part names in the picture below are placeholders for the actual parts that were used.

NASA Mining Competition

Sept 2018 - May 2020

3x Nat'l Champion & Mechanical Team Lead

The yearly NASA Robotic Mining Competition is held by NASA and invites 50 top universities to build the most effective robot that can reach certain targets. The objectives when I participated revolved around autonomy and reaching simulated 'icy regolith' far below the surface (two times the height of the robot and further). NASA uses this competition to source ideas for their own robot, so I like to think my designs have influenced their official projects.


During my freshman year, I designed and introduced a mechanical design that tripled average production (it also doubled the entire competition's single best run) and firmly placed the University of Alabama in first place. The next two years NASA introduced some serious changes, but the team stayed on top. During my second year, most of the senior members had graduated, so it was only one other sophomore and I were doing 80% of the work. By my third year on the team, I was the Mechanical Team lead and our robot had doubled it's production from before and shrunk to a third of size.

Various Projects in C

June 2019 - Aug 2019

After taking a class on C, I was heavily interested in learning more about it. During my Co-op at Quincy Compressor, I spent a significant amount of time coding after work. Along with several small practice projects, I coded a character generation program using ncurses. It guides the user through a process that generates qualities for a character in a book, movie or other medium. The goal was to make interesting, non-cliche characters by enforcing a point system. If the character is amazing and has tons of positive qualities (charming, intelligent, beautiful etc.) then it must also have some bad qualities to round it out. However, rather than just a simple text editor, the program reacted to trends in quality selection to generate some additional qualities. Sometimes restrictions were made on selections, sometimes restrictions were lifted, sometimes restrictions were arbitrarily added. I thoroughly enjoyed the creativity of the characters that resulted and I still like ncurses as a library despite how old it is.
